## Analyze where you are
As a first thing, you need to understand what country you are in and of the crime you are accused of, 
if you are in the EU it will be difficult to apply for extradition since EUROPOL extends the arrest warrant to all local law enforcement agencies, 
a key point to keep in mind is to choose countries/regimes not viewed favorably by your original country, 
if you are in the EU the U.S. influence is particularly heavy that you are forced to apply for extradition to countries such as Russia, Syria and Azerbaijan.

From my point of view the best choice is Azerbaijan (https://rm.coe.int/CoERMPublicCommonSearchServices/DisplayDCTMContent?documentId=0900001680089ae6),
you can easily look for other alternatives here (https://www.coe.int/en/web/transnational-criminal-justice-pcoc/extradition-country-information).
Consider also that nationality bar is the refusal of a state to extradite its ‘own’ nationals.

## Extradition could be denied if...
As one proceeds with the extradition some important policy points have to be respected:

- the sentence for which the extradition is required does not have to be opposed to the;
- fundamental principals of the Italian legal order;
- in cases where crimes require extradition one has to guarantee detention in line with the principal of respecting fundamental rights,
- it should be used in cases of political crimes.
- it should not be used for the death penalty it
- it has to be announced abroad, an irrevocable sentence of guilt or there has to be substantial evidence of guilt,
the request does not have to be added to a criminal case in progress, or a pronounced sentence, in the Italian state (principal of the ne bis in idem: not two times for the same thing).
